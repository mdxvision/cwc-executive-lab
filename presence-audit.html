Executive Presence Audit

Module 2: Master Your Executive Presence

Worksheet

How to Use This Audit

Rate yourself honestly on each behavior from 1-5. There are no "right" answers—this is about understanding where you are today so you can be intentional about where you're going. Your lowest-scoring pillar is your biggest opportunity.

1 = Never/Rarely 2 = Sometimes 3 = Often 4 = Usually 5 = Always/Consistently
G

Gravitas

Behavior Rating
Composure Under Pressure
I remain calm and collected when facing challenges, criticism, or high-stakes situations
Decisiveness
I make decisions confidently and stand by them, even with incomplete information
Comfortable with Silence
I can sit in silence without rushing to fill the space; I use pauses strategically
Physical Presence
I take up appropriate space, sit at the table (not against the wall), and carry myself with confidence
Confidence Without Arrogance
I project self-assurance while remaining open to other perspectives and feedback
C

Communication

Behavior Rating
Clear and Concise
I communicate my ideas clearly, leading with the headline and avoiding over-explanation
Authoritative Language
I speak in statements rather than questions; I avoid hedge words like "just," "I think maybe"
Active Listening
I listen to understand, not to respond; I ask thoughtful follow-up questions
Speaking Up
I contribute my perspective in meetings, even when I'm not asked directly
Vocal Variety
I vary my tone, pace, and volume intentionally; I don't use upspeak
A

Appearance

Behavior Rating
Role-Appropriate Presentation
My visual presentation matches (or exceeds) the expectations of my current role
Intentional Visual Brand
My appearance communicates something specific about who I am as a leader
Consistent Professional Image
I maintain a consistent look that people recognize and associate with me
Authentic Expression
My appearance reflects my authentic self rather than just conforming to expectations
Confidence in Presentation
I feel confident and comfortable in how I present myself visually

Reflection

Which pillar is your strongest?
