The Leadership Shift

What got you here won't get you there. Moving into leadership means your success is measured not by what you do, but by what your team accomplishes.

Individual Contributor Mindset

  • Your expertise is the value
  • You do the work
  • You solve problems
  • Success = your output
  • Recognition for your skills

Leader Mindset

  • Your people are the value
  • You enable the work
  • You develop problem-solvers
  • Success = team's output
  • Recognition for developing others

The Art of Delegation

Delegation isn't about offloading work‚Äîit's about developing your team while freeing yourself for higher-level impact.

1

Delegate Outcomes, Not Tasks

Tell them what success looks like, not exactly how to get there. "I need the client presentation ready by Friday that demonstrates ROI" vs. "Use slide template X and include charts A, B, C."

2

Match to Development Needs

Consider what each person needs to grow. Stretch assignments build capability. Delegate to develop, not just to distribute.

3

Provide Air Cover

Give them authority to act and protect them from organizational interference. "I've asked Maria to lead this. Please direct questions to her."

4

Let Go of Your Way

Their approach may differ from yours. If the outcome is good, the method doesn't matter. Resist the urge to micromanage.

Giving Effective Feedback

The SBI Framework

S

Situation

When & where

B

Behavior

What you observed

I

Impact

The effect it had

Positive Example

S: "In yesterday's client meeting..."

B: "...when you proactively addressed their concerns about timeline..."

I: "...it built their confidence and moved us closer to signing."

Developmental Example

S: "In the team meeting this morning..."

B: "...when you interrupted Sarah twice during her update..."

I: "...it discouraged her from sharing and set a tone that shut others down."

The 70-20-10 Model

How adults actually develop‚Äîuse this to plan meaningful growth experiences:

70%

On-the-Job

Stretch assignments, new projects, cross-functional work, leading initiatives

20%

From Others

Coaching, mentoring, feedback, shadowing, peer learning

10%

Training, courses, certifications, conferences, reading

The Implication

Don't just send people to training. Create real work experiences where they can practice and grow. The job is the classroom.

Creating Stretch Assignments

A stretch assignment should be challenging enough to require growth but achievable with support. Too easy = no growth. Too hard = set up to fail.

Types of Stretches

  • Scope stretch: Larger project or budget
  • Visibility stretch: Present to senior leaders
  • Complexity stretch: Cross-functional work
  • Ambiguity stretch: Undefined problem
  • People stretch: Lead a team or difficult person

Setting Up for Success

  • Explain why you chose them
  • Define success clearly
  • Identify resources and support
  • Set check-in points
  • Create psychological safety to fail

Building Team Culture

Creating an environment where people thrive

Psychological Safety

"Psychological safety is the belief that you won't be punished or humiliated for speaking up with ideas, questions, concerns, or mistakes."
‚Äî Amy Edmondson, Harvard Business School

Signs of Low Safety

  • People stay silent in meetings
  • Mistakes are hidden
  • No one challenges ideas
  • Innovation stagnates
  • Turnover increases

How Leaders Create Safety

  • Model vulnerability‚Äîadmit your mistakes
  • Ask questions more than give answers
  • Respond well to bad news
  • Celebrate learning from failures
  • Protect those who speak up

The Multiplier Effect

When you develop and promote diverse talent, you change not just individual careers but organizational culture.

Managing Performance Issues

Address issues early and directly. Delayed feedback becomes unfair feedback.

1

Diagnose the Root Cause

Is it skill (can't do), will (won't do), or circumstance (blocked from doing)? Your approach depends on the cause.

2

Have the Direct Conversation

Use SBI to share observations. Ask their perspective. Listen. Be clear about expectations and consequences.

3

Create a Clear Plan

Define specific changes needed, support you'll provide, timeline, and how you'll measure progress.

4

Follow Through

Check in regularly. Recognize improvement. If no change, escalate appropriately. Document everything.

When Authority Is Questioned

Some team members may question your authority‚Äîwhether consciously or not. Respond with confidence, consistency, and competence.

Common Scenarios

  • Team member goes around you to your boss
  • Passive resistance to your decisions
  • Testing your boundaries repeatedly
  • Undermining you in meetings
  • Questioning your qualifications

Your Response

  • Address it directly: Name the behavior privately
  • Set clear expectations: "I expect you to come to me first"
  • Be consistent: Same standards for everyone
  • Document patterns: Build your case
  • Escalate if needed: Partner with HR
