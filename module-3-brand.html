Module 3
The Challenge

Why WOC Leaders Need
Intentional Branding

Excellence alone doesn't create recognition. For WOC, being "good at your job" often means being invisible. Your brand closes the gap between your impact and your recognition.

The Visibility Tax

You do the work, others get the credit. Without a clear brand, your contributions disappear.

The Assumption Gap

Others assume what you're capable of based on bias. Your brand pre-corrects those assumptions.

The Opportunity Filter

Decision-makers remember brands, not resumes. If you're not top of mind, you're not in the running.

Module 3
Foundation

What Is a Personal Brand?

Your brand is not a logo or a tagline. It's the consistent experience people have of you—the story they tell themselves and others about who you are and what you deliver.

Reputation + Visibility = Brand

Your brand answers three questions: What are you known for? Who knows it? And what do they do with that knowledge?

Module 3
Framework

Finding Your Brand Sweet Spot

Your most powerful brand lives at the intersection of three elements. When all three align, you're unstoppable.

Your Strengths

What you're genuinely great at—the skills, experiences, and capabilities where you have proven expertise.

Your Passions

What energizes you—the work that doesn't feel like work, the problems you love to solve.

Market Needs

What's valued—the problems organizations pay to solve, the gaps that need filling.

Module 3
Current State

Your Brand Audit

Before building, take stock. What's your brand today—whether intentional or not?

Questions to Ask Yourself

  • What am I most often asked to help with?
  • What do people praise me for?
  • What feedback do I get repeatedly?
  • What do colleagues introduce me as?
  • What would my LinkedIn say if I were honest?

Questions to Ask Others

  • What 3 words would you use to describe me?
  • What do you think I'm best at?
  • What's my superpower?
  • What would you recommend me for?
  • What do you think I should be known for?

Module 3
Positioning

The Brand Positioning Statement

A clear positioning statement makes it easy for others to understand—and remember—what you bring. Use this formula to draft yours.

I help [target audience]
achieve [outcome/transformation]
by [your unique approach/method]
which results in [concrete value/impact]

Example: I help tech startups scale their engineering teams by building inclusive hiring systems, which results in 40% faster hiring and doubled retention.

Module 3
Differentiation

What Makes You Different?

Your differentiator isn't "working hard" or "being a team player." It's the unique combination of skills, experiences, and perspectives only you bring.

Your Unique Combination

What 2-3 skills or experiences do you bring together that few others have? (e.g., engineering + design + leadership)

Your Signature Story

What challenge have you overcome that gives you unique credibility? Your background is an asset.

Your Perspective

What do you see that others miss? What contrarian or uncommon view do you hold?

Your Results

What outcomes do you consistently deliver? What's your track record of impact?

Module 3
Identity + Brand

Your Identity as an Asset

Your lived experience as a WOC isn't separate from your professional brand—it's part of what makes you valuable. The question is how to leverage it strategically.

What Your Experience Brings

  • Code-switching fluency = adaptability
  • Navigating bias = resilience + strategy
  • Community perspective = inclusive innovation
  • Over-preparation habit = excellence standard
  • Outsider insight = fresh solutions

Positioning Your Identity

  • Lead with expertise, not identity alone
  • Choose when and how much to share
  • Let your work create the opening
  • Build bridges through shared challenges
  • Use your voice for change when ready

Module 3
Amplification

The Visibility Strategy

A brand nobody sees isn't a brand. You need a strategy for getting your brand in front of the right people.

Internal Visibility

Speaking up in meetings, volunteering for visible projects, sharing wins with your manager, building relationships with sponsors.

Industry Visibility

Conference speaking, writing articles, participating in panels, joining professional organizations, building a network of peers.

Digital Visibility

LinkedIn presence, thought leadership posts, engaging with industry conversations, building your online portfolio.

Module 3
Internal Visibility

Building Your Brand Inside Your Organization

Before going external, maximize your internal visibility. This is where promotions actually happen.

Share Your Work

Send regular updates to your manager. Highlight impact, not just activity. Make your wins visible.

Seek Visibility Projects

Volunteer for cross-functional initiatives. Present to leadership. Lead something new.

Build Strategic Relationships

Connect with leaders outside your direct chain. Build relationships before you need them.

Position Yourself for What's Next

Talk about your aspirations. Make sure decision-makers know what you want.

Module 3
Digital Presence

Your LinkedIn as Brand Real Estate

For professionals, LinkedIn is the most important digital brand platform. Make it work for you.

Optimize Your Profile

  • Headline: Brand statement, not job title
  • About: Story + value + call to action
  • Experience: Impact, not responsibilities
  • Photo: Professional but approachable
  • Banner: Reinforce your brand

Build Visibility

  • Post 1-2x per week minimum
  • Share insights, not just achievements
  • Comment thoughtfully on others' posts
  • Engage with your target audience
  • Accept connection requests strategically
Reflection
