Career Paths Aren't Linear

The corporate ladder is a myth. Modern careers are more like a jungle gym‚Äîyou move sideways, diagonally, sometimes even backwards to go forward.

Vertical Move

Traditional promotion‚Äîsame function, higher level. More scope, more responsibility, more influence.

‚ÜîÔ∏è

Lateral Move

Same level, different function or business. Builds breadth, expands skills, creates new opportunities.

‚ÜóÔ∏è

Diagonal Move

Different function AND higher level. Often requires going external. High risk, high reward.

Consider All Options

Sometimes the fastest path to your goal isn't a straight line. A lateral move into a high-growth area might accelerate your career more than waiting for a promotion.

Stay or Go?

Be honest about whether your current organization can support your vision:

Reasons to Stay

  • Clear path to your next role
  • Strong sponsorship in place
  • Company growing / opportunities expanding
  • Culture supports your success
  • You're learning and challenged
  • Compensation is competitive

Signals to Explore

  • Path blocked or unclear
  • No one advocating for you
  • Company shrinking / layoffs
  • Bias limiting your growth
  • Stagnant‚Äînot learning
  • Significantly underpaid vs. market

The Smart Play

Always know your market value. Always have relationships outside your company. You don't have to leave‚Äîbut you should always have options. Leverage comes from alternatives.

What Does Success Really Mean?

"Success is not the key to happiness. Happiness is the key to success. If you love what you are doing, you will be successful."
‚Äî Albert Schweitzer

External Success Markers

  • Title and compensation
  • Scope of responsibility
  • Industry recognition
  • Team size / budget
  • Status and prestige

Internal Success Markers

  • Alignment with values
  • Meaningful impact
  • Joy in daily work
  • Time for what matters
  • Health and wellbeing

The goal isn't to choose one or the other‚Äîit's to be intentional about which metrics matter to YOU and to make tradeoffs consciously.

Protecting What Matters

Ambition without boundaries leads to burnout. Sustainable success requires protecting time for rest, relationships, and renewal.

Energy Management
