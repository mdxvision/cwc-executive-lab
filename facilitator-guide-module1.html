2-3 min

Talking Points

  • Here's the uncomfortable truth: You've been told to put your head down and deliver. But excellence alone doesn't create advancement.
  • Let me break down the three gaps that keep high performers stuck:
  • The Visibility Gap: Your work speaks for itself - but it's speaking to the wrong people, or not at all. If decision-makers don't know your name, they can't advocate for you.
  • The Hustle Trap: More work won't get you promoted. I've seen women take on more and more, thinking that's the path forward. It's not. Strategic positioning will get you further than working harder.

Key Message: Performance is the baseline. Promotion requires visibility, positioning, and sponsorship.
5
Framework: The Three Career Plateaus
2-3 min

Talking Points

  • Every plateau falls into one of three categories. Understanding yours is the first step to breaking through.
  • Skill Plateau: You've maxed out your current capabilities. Growth requires new skills, experiences, or credentials to reach the next level.
  • Role Plateau: Your position has a ceiling. There's no room to grow - limited scope or organizational constraints are blocking advancement.
  • Identity Plateau: This one is internal. Imposter syndrome, perfectionism, or playing small are holding you back from stepping into your power.
  • Most people experience a combination of these - but there's usually one primary plateau that's driving the others.
  • Let's explore each one so you can identify where you are.
Transition: "Let's start with the Skill Plateau..."
6
Deep Dive: Skill Plateau
2 min

Talking Points

  • The Skill Plateau happens when your expertise brought you here, but it won't take you there.
  • Signs you're here: You're known as "the expert" but you keep getting passed over for leadership. Your strengths feel outdated or too narrow. You lack exposure to strategic work. Your comfort zone has become a ceiling.
  • The breakthrough: Identify 2-3 skills needed for your next role. Seek stretch assignments, not just promotions. Build cross-functional exposure. Reposition your brand around future value, not just past achievements.
  • Ask yourself: Am I known for what I've done, or what I can do next?
7
Deep Dive: Role Plateau
2 min

Talking Points

  • The Role Plateau happens when the role itself has no runway - it's not your performance.
  • Signs you're here: Your manager has nowhere to go either. The org is flat or restructuring. Budget constraints limit new positions. You've outgrown the scope but can't expand.
  • The breakthrough: Explore lateral moves with growth potential. Consider adjacent departments or functions. Build visibility outside your team. And sometimes, you need to know when it's time to pivot externally.
  • This plateau often requires the hardest realization: the opportunity you want may not exist in your current environment.
8
Deep Dive: Identity Plateau
2 min

Talking Points

  • The Identity Plateau is when the barrier isn't skill or opportunity - it's self-perception.
  • Signs you're here: Imposter syndrome despite your track record. Perfectionism that delays action. Fear of visibility or failure. Shrinking to fit instead of standing out.
  • The breakthrough: Challenge the internal narrative. Take "messy action" - done beats perfect. Build evidence of your capability. Step into visibility before you feel ready.
  • I want you to really sit with this one. Many of us have internalized messages about who gets to lead, who gets to be visible, who gets to take up space. Those messages are not facts - they're stories we can rewrite.
Key Message: The identity plateau is often the hardest to see because it feels like truth, not limitation.

Root Causes: What's Driving Your Plateau?
2-3 min

Talking Points

  • Beyond the three plateau types, there are specific root causes that keep us stuck. See which of these resonate:
  • Unclear Target: No defined "next best role" or direction makes it impossible to move strategically. If you don't know where you're going, you can't build a path.
  • Unclear Value Proposition: You can't articulate your unique value or what you bring that others don't.
  • Low Visibility: Working hard but decision-makers don't know your name or your impact.
  • Weak Sponsorship: No one with power is advocating for you when opportunities arise.
  • Over-Functioning: So busy doing everyone's work that there's no bandwidth for your own growth.
  • Fear and Avoidance: Avoiding the conversations, risks, or visibility that advancement requires.
  • As I read through these, which ones hit home for you?

12
Pattern Recognition: The Stuck Loop
2-3 min

Talking Points

  • Most plateaus follow a predictable pattern. Breaking free starts with recognizing yours.
  • Here's how the Stuck Loop works: A Trigger happens, which creates a Thought, which creates an Emotion, which drives a Behavior, which produces an Outcome - and that outcome reinforces the original pattern.
  • Let me give you an example: You're asked to present to leadership. The thought is "I'll mess this up." That creates anxiety. So you over-prepare, speak fast, keep it safe. The outcome? It's "fine" but you remain invisible. And that reinforces the original doubt.
  • The loop continues until we consciously interrupt it.
  • In a moment, you're going to identify YOUR stuck loop.
13
Reflection: Map Your Stuck Loop
8-10 min

Talking Points

  • Now it's your turn. Pull up your Stuck Loop worksheet.
  • You're going to do three things:
  • Step 1: Identify Your Loop. What trigger, thought, emotion, behavior, and outcome keep repeating in your career?
  • Step 2: Design Your New Choice. What new thought or behavior could interrupt this pattern? Where can you make a different decision?
  • Step 3: Name Your Support. What reminder, tool, or accountability would help you make that different choice?
  • Take 5-7 minutes for this. Be specific - vague answers won't help you break the pattern.

Section 4 Strategic Planning (Slides 14-15)
14
Strategic Planning: Your Career Growth Roadmap
3-4 min

Talking Points

  • Now we're going to build your Career Growth Roadmap - a structured approach to move from where you are to where you want to be.
  • This roadmap has six steps, and they need to be done in order:
  • Step 1 - North Star: Define your "next best role" and what success looks like. This is your destination.
  • Step 2 - Current Reality: An honest assessment of where you are right now and what's blocking progress.
  • Step 3 - Strengths & Gaps: What do you leverage? What do you need to develop for the next level?
  • Step 4 - 90-Day Goals: Break this down into 30-day, 60-day, and 90-day milestones with clear success measures.
  • Step 5 - First Moves: Concrete actions for the next 14 days to build momentum.
  • Step 6 - Accountability: Who will hold you to this and how often will you check in?
  • This document becomes your north star for the year.
15
Deep Work: Build Your Roadmap
25-30 min

Talking Points

  • This is the deep work. Pull up your Career Growth Roadmap worksheet.

  • What is my next best role? What must be true in 6-12 months? What are my top 3 strengths? What 2 gaps must I close? What 3 actions can I start this week?
  • A few reminders as you work:
  • Be specific - "get promoted" isn't a goal. Make it measurable - how will you know you've succeeded?
  • Focus on what you can influence. Name real barriers - they're data, not excuses.

Relationship Capital: Map Your Network
8-10 min

Talking Points

  • Before building your network, you need to take stock. Who currently influences your growth?
  • Your Relationship Baseline Map helps you identify six categories of relationships:
  • Manager/Direct Leader: Who directly influences your development and reviews?
  • Key Stakeholders: 2-3 people whose opinion shapes your reputation.
  • Potential Sponsors: Who has power and might advocate for you behind closed doors?
  • Mentors: Who provides guidance, wisdom, or career advice?
  • Peer Allies: Trusted peers you collaborate with and lean on.
  • External Community: Networks and groups outside your organization.
  • Take a few minutes to map your current network. This baseline will reveal gaps and opportunities.

Momentum: The 14-Day Sprint
5-7 min

Talking Points

Section 6 Summary & Next Steps (Slides 19-21)
19
Key Takeaways
2-3 min

Talking Points

  • Let's recap the four key takeaways from today:
  • 1. Plateaus Are Diagnosable. Skill, Role, or Identity - knowing your type unlocks the right strategy. You can't fix what you can't name.
  • 2. Hard Work Alone Won't Get You There. Strategic positioning, visibility, and sponsorship matter as much as performance. Excellence is the baseline, not the differentiator.
  • 3. Patterns Repeat Until Interrupted. Your stuck loop will continue until you make a different choice. Awareness is the first step to change.
  • 4. Momentum Beats Planning. 14 days of action creates more clarity than 90 days of thinking. Start before you're ready.
